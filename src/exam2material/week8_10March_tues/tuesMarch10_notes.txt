Reminder:

AllE - when can we use?
    a for all statement 
    individual (either a parameter to proof function OR 
        introduced in a subproof)


AllI - when do we use? idea?
    when we want to create/introduce a for all statement, use AllI 
    involves a subproof
        name a random individual in my domain at beginning of subproof
            (must be an individual I haven't used before)
        by end of subproof: whatever statement I want for everyone,
            be able to make that statement about my random individual
        right after subproof: introduce same statement with a for all
